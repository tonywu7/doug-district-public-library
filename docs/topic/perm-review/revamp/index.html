[hide menu]

Updated guiding principles

  • Single source of truth within same access level. Whenever possible, a permission for a member should be granted by at most one role or channel role override per access level.
  • Isolation between access levels. A permission granted to a member at a lower access level should be granted again on additional higher access levels. This is so that a change to permissions to lower levels (such as regular members) do not affect access by higher level members (such as Mods).
  • Just enough access. A member should not have more permissions than it need. This includes bots.

Access levels

  1. Unverified @everyone
  2. Regular @Dannyling
  3. Privileged @Spoonie @Twitch VIP @Pepto Bismol Spoonie @Gaming God @Founder
  4. Moderator @Mod @Twitch Mod @Discord Mod @Comm Manager
  5. Administrator @Admin
  6. *Non-human @Bot

The grammar police patch

Change ?iagreeonrules to ?iagreetorules. "to agree on" means to come to a consensus about something, new member do not negotiate with us about the rules, we define the rules and they accept the rules, hence it should be "to agree to": to accedes to, to accept, to comply with

Pending a better use of the @Undoubted role, rename it to "Trusted," or some other themed/non-meaningful name, say "Bernie" or "Barack", then explain what it does in the role wiki.
"Undoubted" means "indisputable," "incontrovertible," "unquestionable." It does not communicate clearly what it does, is not something we want to express, (you can't say that a member is "unquestionable," not even the mods are unquestionable), is infrequently used, and is confusing for new members.

Singularize role names. Role names indicate the identity of each member and should be read like so:

umbu is a (singular) @Comm Manager, a @Discord Mod, a @Twitch Mod, a @Dannyling

Internal operations

These discussions address mod operations.

@Discord Mod as the only role members may use to reach the mod team.

Currently, members may notify the mod team via any of the following roles: @Twitch Mod, @Discord Mod, @Comm Manager, @Admin

Per latest (as of May 23, 2021) discussions in #mods-serious, there exists very little reason for members to notify @Admin. In almost all cases, for urgent matters, @Discord Mod or @Comm Manager will suffice.
Thus allowing members to ping @Admin opens up spaces for misuse, abuse, and misjudged/unnecessary punishment.
